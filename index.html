How Travel Rewards, Points & Miles Actually Work

TravelRewards.Creditcard explains how to earn, transfer and redeem travel points and miles with maximum value — without falling for low-value redemptions.

Compare travel-reward cards

Travel Rewards 101

Travel-reward credit cards earn points or miles that can be exchanged for:

  • Flights (economy → business upgrades)
  • Hotels & resort stays
  • Rental cars
  • Experiences
  • Cashback alternatives

The real value lies in miles and transfer partners — not in fixed-value redemptions.

How Travel Points & Miles Are Earned

Earning rates vary massively depending on card type, category bonus and loyalty ecosystem:

  • Travel spending: 2×–5× multipliers
  • Dining: often 2×–3× across many cards
  • Airline/hotel co-brands: very high multipliers with the same brand
  • Welcome bonuses: major boost early in membership
  • Transfer bonuses: seasonal 10–40% boosts

High-value travel cards combine strong earn rates with good transfer flexibility.

Transfer Partners: Where the Real Value Is

Transferable points ecosystems usually offer the highest redemption value:

  • Major airline alliances (Star Alliance, oneworld, SkyTeam)
  • Hotel programs (Hilton, Marriott, IHG)
  • Regional airline sweet spots

Instead of redeeming points at 0.5–1 cent fixed value, transfers can yield 2–8 cents per point on premium flights.

Redemption Pitfalls to Avoid

Low-value redemptions that most beginners fall into:

  • Gift cards (typically low fixed value)
  • Retail purchases via “shop with points”
  • Low-tier hotel redemptions without good valuation
  • Dynamic pricing with poor prices during peak demand

Your reward value depends entirely on how you redeem — not how you earn.
